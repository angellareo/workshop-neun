About the Workshop

Computational neuroscience bridges the gap between experimental observations and theoretical understanding of neural systems. This workshop will guide you through:

  1. Theoretical Foundations: Understanding why and how to model neural systems
  2. Practical Skills: Learning to use the Neun library for neural simulations
  3. Real Applications: Create your own models and circuits!

The Neun Library

Neun is a C++ library for simulating neuronal networks. You can directly work in C++ or use our Python bindings to directly simulate your models in Python.

Workshop Structure

Part 1: Introduction to Computational Neuroscience

Learn the fundamental principles of neural modeling, from single neurons to networks. Understand the mathematical foundations and their biological relevance.

Duration: ~20 min

Topics:

  • Why model neural systems?
  • Single neuron dynamics
  • From neurons to networks

Part 2: Hands-on with Neun to simulate neural dynamics and networks

Get practical experience building and simulating neural models using the Neun library.

Duration: ~3 h

Topics:

  • Installing and setting up Neun
  • Creating single neuron models
  • Building and simulating neural networks
  • Advanced modeling techniques
  • Analyzing the results

Part 3: Conclusions and closing

Duration: ~20 min

Topics: - Analyzing neural dynamics

Prerequisites

To get the most out of this workshop, you should have:

  • Required: Basic Python programming skills
  • Recommended: Experience with NumPy and Matplotlib

In your system, please ensure you have:

  1. Python 3.7 or higher, with development headers, installed on your computer
  2. C++ compiler with C++20 support (GCC 10+, Clang 10+)
